# Crime and Economic Indicators Correlation Analysis

## Project Overview

This project analyzes the relationship between organized crime rates and key economic indicators across countries. The study explores how criminality levels correlate with GDP per capita, exports, and imports using statistical methods such as correlation, covariance, and scatter plot analysis.

## Problem Statement

The objective is to understand whether economic activity influences organized crime levels across countries. By examining relationships between criminality averages and economic variables, the study attempts to identify economic patterns associated with crime rates.

## Objectives

* Analyze the relationship between criminality rates and GDP.
* Study the correlation between criminality and exports.
* Evaluate the relationship between criminality and imports.
* Identify which economic factor has the strongest influence on crime.

## Dataset Variables

The dataset includes the following variables:

* Criminality Rate (crime per 100,000 people)
* GDP per Capita
* Exports
* Imports

## Methodology

### Data Preparation

* Data collected from international sources
* Cleaning and removing inconsistencies
* Organizing data by country

### Statistical Techniques Used

* Correlation Analysis
* Covariance Analysis
* Scatter Plot Visualization

## Key Results

Correlation Results:

* Criminality vs Export: **0.106 (Weak Positive Correlation)**
* Criminality vs Import: **0.116 (Weak Positive Correlation)**
* Criminality vs GDP per Capita: **-0.293 (Weak Negative Correlation)**

Covariance Results:

* Criminality & Export → Positive relationship
* Criminality & Import → Positive relationship
* Criminality & GDP → Negative relationship

## Key Insights

* Countries with higher exports tend to show slightly higher organized crime levels.
* Import levels also show a weak positive relationship with crime.
* Higher GDP per capita tends to reduce criminality levels.
