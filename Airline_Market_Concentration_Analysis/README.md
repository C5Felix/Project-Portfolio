# Airline Market Concentration Analysis (HHI)

## Project Overview

This project analyzes the market structure and competition trends in the Indian airline industry using the Herfindahl–Hirschman Index (HHI). The analysis evaluates market concentration levels and identifies whether the industry operates under competitive or oligopolistic conditions.

## Objective

* Measure market concentration in the Indian airline industry.
* Analyze competition trends among major airlines.
* Interpret the structure of the aviation market using economic indicators.

## Methodology

The Herfindahl–Hirschman Index (HHI) is used to measure market concentration.

HHI is calculated as the sum of the squares of the market shares of all firms in the industry.

Interpretation of HHI:

* HHI < 1500 → Competitive Market
* 1500 – 2500 → Moderately Concentrated Market
* HHI > 2500 → Highly Concentrated Market

## Market Share Data

IndiGo – 61.6%
Air India – 13.7%
Vistara – 9.2%
Akasa Air – 4.8%
SpiceJet – 4.4%
Go First – 0.4%

## Result

HHI Index = **4109.45**

This indicates that the Indian airline industry is **highly concentrated**, dominated by a few major airlines.

## Market Structure

The airline industry in India operates as an **oligopoly**, characterized by:

* Few dominant players
* High barriers to entry
* Interdependent pricing strategies

## Key Insights

* IndiGo holds the largest domestic market share.
* Government policies such as UDAN influence competition.
* Infrastructure and fuel costs affect airline profitability.
